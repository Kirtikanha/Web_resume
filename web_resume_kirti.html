Research Project

Prediction and Correlation of Blood Glucose and Hematocrit levels Using an Artificial Neural Network on Amperometric Signals

  • Developed a research project focused on estimating blood glucose concentration and hematocrit volume using a neural network model based on amperometric signals.
  • Collected and processed a large dataset of amperometric signals from an electrochemical glucose sensor.
  • Designed and trained a neural network model, employing artificial neural network regressors, to estimate blood glucose concentration and hematocrit volume based on the extracted features.
  • Optimized the neural network model using hyperparameter tuning techniques to enhance the accuracy and robustness of the predictions.
  • Validated the model's performance by comparing the estimated blood glucose concentration and hematocrit volume with reference values obtained from clinical measurements.
  • Achieved significant correlation between the estimated values and the reference values, demonstrating the efficacy of the neural network model in accurately estimating blood glucose concentration and hematocrit volume.

This project demonstrates my ability to apply machine learning techniques, specifically neural networks, in the domain of biomedical engineering. It showcases my skills in model development, optimization, and result validation. Additionally, the presentation and report writing skills exhibited throughout the project demonstrate effective communication of complex technical concepts.
